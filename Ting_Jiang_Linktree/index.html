Hi there, I'm Ting Jiang, a behavioral scientist, systems thinker, and entrepreneur who believes that business can be a vehicle for human healing.


After many years working at the intersection of behavioral economics and social impact, I went through a period of burnout that, instead of breaking me, opened me up. It led to a deeper awakening — a realization that science alone could not address the full spectrum of human experience.


So I began a new path, one that merged science with soul. I immersed myself in bodywork, spiritual teachings, and energy practices — not to turn away from behavioral science, but to deepen and perfect it.


That journey gave birth to Hummingbird Habits — a space dedicated to reimagining the products and services we use every day as tools for care, clarity, and transformation.


Before founding HHT, I was one of the principals at the Dan Ariely Behavioral Science Lab at Duke University. Today, I continue to bridge science and human insight as the Founder and CEO of Hummingbird Habits, Adjunct Chief Science Officer of Make-A-Point, and Advisor to The Decision Lab.

Helping businesses turn every touchpoint into a moment of care, clarity and connection.


Empowering the companies that shape our daily lives to serve the highest level of human needs. And all this does not go against business — in fact, more signs point that it is the future of business:

  • Today's consumers are not just looking for products, but healing, wholeness, and inner consistency.
  • The global health and healing industry is growing rapidly.
  • This is not a fad, but a profound change.
